## 3.3 The *A. baumannii* Quorum-Sensing Molecular Network

QS in *A. baumannii* is a multi-layered web rather than a single LuxI/LuxR circuit. The AHL axis remains the canonical core, but contemporary work integrates it with an indole receptor system, three nucleotide second-messenger pools, several two-component systems (TCSs), and accessory regulators (AbaM, ABUW_1132, CavA/VfrAb, DksA) that coordinate biofilm, efflux, surface and motility phenotypes [Cui 2025_2; Niu 2008]. Extended structural and mechanistic detail is provided in Supplementary S9.1.

### 3.3.1 The *abaI*/*abaR* axis

Identified by Niu and colleagues in *A. baumannii* M2 [Niu 2008], AbaI is the LuxI-family AHL synthase and AbaR the cognate LuxR-family receptor. A *lux*-box-like sequence upstream of *abaI* mediates a positive-feedback loop in which AHL-bound AbaR drives further *abaI* transcription [Cui 2025_2]. The *abaI*::Km mutant lacks detectable AHLs and shows ~40% biofilm reduction, attenuated motility and virulence in *Galleria* and murine models, and increased serum susceptibility [Niu 2008; Sun 2021_2]. The AbaR regulon spans capsule, siderophore and biofilm loci well beyond *abaI* [Sun 2021]. AbaM brakes AHL output and uncouples QS-driven biofilm from systemic virulence [Lpez-Martn 2021]. The LysR-type modulator ABUW_1132 positively regulates *abaIR*; its deletion switches the VIR-O virulent variant to AV-T avirulent [Tierney 2021]. Endogenous CRISPR-Cas activity can target *abaI* and modulate antibiotic susceptibility [Wang 2022_2].

### 3.3.2 AHL signal chemistry

N-(3-hydroxydodecanoyl)-L-homoserine lactone (3-OH-C12-HSL) is the dominant AHL, accompanied by minor 3-OH-C10-HSL and 3-oxo-C11-HSL species [Niu 2008]. Profiles converge on medium- to long-chain 3-hydroxy structures across clinical and environmental isolates [Gonzlez 2009; Mayer 2018]. LC-MS/MS quantification has demonstrated 3-OH-C12-HSL in plasma from burn patients with *A. baumannii* septic shock [Carpenito 2025]. Atypically for LuxR systems, AbaR responds nearly identically to (R)- and (S)-3-OH-C12-HSL (EC50 0.67 and 0.82 μM) [Garner 2012; Stacy 2012]. Synthetic AHL analogues with aromatic head-groups or triazole linkages function as AbaR antagonists [Stacy 2012_2]. Signalling is environmentally tunable: blue light reduces *abaI* ~38-fold, and human serum albumin sequesters 3-OH-C12-HSL [Tuttobene 2021; Pimentel 2021]. AHL pools can be erased enzymatically by lactonases (MomL, AaL, AidA) and the *P. aeruginosa* acylase PvdQ [Bergonzi 2018; Lpez 2017_2; Vogel 2022].

### 3.3.3 Cross-regulation with two-component systems

BfmRS is the best-characterised QS partner: BfmR/BfmS expression rises in response to exogenous C6-HSL and tracks up-regulation of the *csuA/BABCDE* chaperone-usher operon [Luo 2015]. BfmRS and AbaIR jointly govern virulence-associated genes [Kim 2022]. Loss of the (p)ppGpp cofactor DksA de-represses *bfmR/S* and *csuC/D/E* [Kim 2021]. AdeRS controls AdeABC RND efflux: ∆*adeB* shows elevated AHL output, ∆*adeRS* shows the strongest motility phenotype, and AdeFGH overexpression couples to AHL-stimulated biofilm [Lpez 2017; Xie 2025; He 2015]. The CheA/Y-like hybrid regulator A1S_2811 is required for AbaIR-dependent motility and biofilm [Chen 2017]. The PmrAB-QS interface remains uncharacterised [YousefiNojookambari 2024].

### 3.3.4 QS-dependent regulons

Four output modules recur. *Biofilm matrix*: AHL signalling and the indole-responsive regulator AbiR converge on the *csu* operon; AbiR loss reduces biofilm by ~41% [Cui 2023; Cui 2025]. Bap and *pgaABCD* (PNAG synthesis) track *abaI/abaR* in clinical isolates [Mendes 2023; Rumbo-Feal 2013]. *Efflux*: AdeFGH overexpression accompanies AHL-driven biofilm induction under sub-MIC antibiotic exposure, linking QS to phenotypic tolerance [He 2015]. *OmpA*: traffics via outer-membrane vesicles to deliver virulence factors and to mediate AHL exchange, with reduced expression in *abaI*-disrupted backgrounds [Vinitha 2025; Kostoulias 2015]. *Iron acquisition*: acinetobactin biosynthesis and BauA-TonB uptake are co-regulated with QS; human pleural fluid and HSA simultaneously down-regulate *abaI*, *abaR* and iron-uptake genes [Ghavanloughajar 2025; Pimentel 2021_2].

### 3.3.5 Nucleotide second messengers

Three nucleotide pools feed into QS. The ATCC 17978 genome encodes 12 GGDEF/EAL proteins; specific diguanylate cyclases and phosphodiesterases modulate biofilm, motility and virulence on deletion, and the c-di-GMP effector EF-P couples this messenger to translational rescue of proline-rich proteins [Cui 2025_2]. (p)ppGpp produced by RelA (ABUW_3302) modulates antibiotic susceptibility and represses ABUW_1132, placing the stringent response upstream of the AHL circuit [Prez-Varela 2020; Tierney 2021]. The adenylate cyclase CavA produces 3′,5′-cAMP, which binds VfrAb; in AB5075, cAMP positively regulates c-di-GMP and represses *abaI*, placing cAMP at the apex of a cascade that integrates density (AHL), energy state ((p)ppGpp) and host cue (cAMP) at a single transcriptional decision point [Harkova 2024].

### 3.3.6 Polymicrobial signal cross-talk

*P. aeruginosa* LasI/RhlI signals modulate *A. baumannii* biofilm in mixed cultures with reciprocal AHL effects [Bhargava 2012]. MvfR (PqsR) is central in co-culture, and pharmacological MvfR inhibition partially restores *A. baumannii* survival without altering *P. aeruginosa* abundance [Wheeler 2025; Peleg 2008]. Indole produced by *A. baumannii* AbiS represses *P. aeruginosa* QS and T3SS, defining a bidirectional axis [Cui 2022; Li 2025]. Carnosol disrupts AbiR-promoter binding (KD = 0.3 μM) and reduces biofilm, motility and cytotoxicity by 25-85% [Cui 2025]. Cross-kingdom interactions with *Candida albicans* facilitate *A. baumannii* pneumonia [Subbarayudu 2024]. Engineered lactonases reduce inter- and intra-species signalling in mixed-species biofilms [Chow 2013; Mayer 2018].

### 3.3.7 Open mechanistic questions

Detailed in S9.1, the principal unresolved issues are: prevalence of functional *abiS*/*abiR* across IC1/IC2 clones [Djahanschiri 2022]; absence of confirmed AI-2/DSF/PQS equivalents; unidentified c-di-GMP effectors beyond EF-P; uncharacterised PmrAB-QS interface; absent in vivo direct evidence for polymicrobial AHL exchange; no genome-wide AbaR ChIP-seq dataset [Sun 2021]; and mechanistically thin links to small RNAs and nucleoid-associated proteins [Krger 2016; Escalante 2022].
