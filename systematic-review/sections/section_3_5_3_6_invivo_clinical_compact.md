## 3.5 In Vivo and Preclinical Evidence

### 3.5.1 Invertebrate models

Invertebrate hosts dominate the preclinical pipeline. Peleg and colleagues established *Galleria mellonella* as a tractable model: killing is inoculum- and temperature-dependent (significantly greater mortality at 37°C than 30°C, P = 0.01) and clinical *A. baumannii* strains outperform less-pathogenic relatives [Peleg_2009]. Notably, genetic reduction of 3-OH-C12-HSL production did **not** alter virulence in *G. mellonella*, an early caution against direct in vitro-to-larval extrapolation. *C. elegans* coinfection assays confirmed mixed-species QS-regulated competition without directly testing survival readouts [Peleg_2008]. Subsequent studies have shown reproducible QS-dependent attenuation: an ATCC 17978 ∆*abaI* mutant produced 30% absolute mortality reduction [FernandezGarcia_2018]; in 80 north-east-China clinical isolates, AHL-producing strains killed significantly more larvae and *abaI/abaR* carriage correlated with MDR (P < 0.01) [Tang_2020]; ∆*abaI* and ∆*abaIR* derivatives showed serum sensitivity and attenuated larval lethality [Sun_2021_2]. Hypervirulent bacteraemic isolates produced zonula occludens toxin in a density-coupled fashion [Benyamini_2024], and community-onset XDR clones showed enhanced larval and murine virulence [Chen_2024].

QSI efficacy in invertebrate models is uneven. Levamisole improved *Galleria*/murine survival from 0% to 60% at 72 h at 1/8 MIC [Beasley_2025; Seleem_2020]. Phytochemicals (peppermint, *Paederia foetida*, carnosol) yielded statistically significant larval-mortality reductions with QS-transcript suppression [Cui_2025; Santajit_2025; MajidiFard_2025]. The 3014/3098 two-phage cocktail produced 86.67% larval survival at 96 h (P < 0.001) against an XDR burn-isolate, far exceeding single-phage performance [deVilliersdelaNoue_2025]. Zebrafish *abaI*-knockout work confirmed attenuated cytokine induction and improved survival [Jiang_2024; Mardiana_2023]. Per-study protocols are tabulated in S9.3.

### 3.5.2 Murine models

Murine evidence concentrates on peritonitis, pneumonia, bacteraemia, burn-wound and biofilm-on-implant infections. The most complete recent QSI-in-mouse dataset comes from Cui and colleagues: 40 BALB/c mice received intraperitoneal *A. baumannii* ATCC 17978 followed by oral carnosol (100 µM) or PBS; day-4 mortality fell from 80% to 30%, with parallel suppression of caspase-1, ASC, NLRP3, IL-1β, IL-6 and TNF-α in macrophages [Cui_2025]. ARRIVE 2.0 adherence was claimed, but sample size was not predetermined statistically. The Wienhold phage pneumonia model is the most methodologically rigorous: C57BL/6N mice received transnasal MDR challenge followed by single intratracheal phage aerosolisation, producing ~1 log10 CFU reduction in lung tissue at 24 h with concordant histopathology and lung-barrier improvement [Wienhold_2021]. Histopathology was evaluated by blinded board-certified pathologists — a rarity. Ex vivo human lung tissue confirmed no detectable CFU at 2 h post-phage without IL-1β/IL-8 induction. Saclayvirus vB_AbaM_P1 prevented murine pneumonia [Li_2024], and inhalable spray-dried phage powders anticipate pulmonary translation [Yan_2021]. Bacteraemia studies demonstrate phage cocktail efficacy against XDR isolates [Leshkasheli_2019], while PvdQ established that exogenous QQ enzymes can attenuate biofilm in vivo [Vogel_2022]. Burn-wound and implant models include hydrogel phages with accelerated dermal collagen [Elshamy_2025], repurposed nitroimidazoles [Khafagy_2025], depolymerase-bearing vB_AbaM_AB4P2 [Su_2025], staphyloxanthin niosomes (68-88% biofilm reduction with QS-transcript suppression) [Nosair_2025] and silver nanoparticles [Hetta_2021]. Single-agent bacterial-burden reductions cluster around 1-2 log10 CFU, with the largest reductions for phage-antibiotic combinations (>6 log10 CFU/mL synergy with colistin and the 3014/3098 cocktail).

### 3.5.3 PK/PD gaps

A systematic deficiency is the absence of pharmacokinetic characterisation for putative QSIs. Carnosol was tested at a single oral dose without plasma exposure or target-tissue data [Cui_2025]. Carpenito's LC-MS/MS plasma method, although developed for QS molecules rather than QSIs, provides the analytical foundation required for QSI PK programmes and demonstrates nanogram-per-millilitre quantification feasibility in human plasma [Carpenito_2025]. No PK/PD study in the corpus has defined AUC/MIC, T>MIC, or equivalent indices for any QSI in *A. baumannii*, rendering human-equivalent-dose prediction impossible.

### 3.5.4 Methodological quality (ARRIVE 2.0)

Reporting quality falls short of ARRIVE 2.0 expectations. Power-calculation-based sample sizing was reported in <10% of in vivo papers. Randomisation was stated in ~one-third of studies and blinding of outcome assessment in <one-quarter. Attrition was inconsistently disclosed. Effect-size estimates from these studies should therefore be treated as exploratory and at substantial risk of overestimation; SYRCLE-based risk-of-bias assessment should precede any future meta-analytic pooling. ## 3.6 Clinical Translational Evidence

### 3.6.1 QS molecules in patient samples

Direct measurement of *A. baumannii* QS molecules in patient material is in its infancy. Carpenito and colleagues developed and validated an LC-MS/MS method for nine QS molecules (five AHLs and four 2-alkyl-4(1H)-quinolones) in human plasma, with LOQs 0.02-0.79 ng/mL and intra-assay CV <15% [Carpenito_2025]. Applied to three critically ill burn patients with multidrug-resistant *A. baumannii* septic shock, 3-OH-C12-HSL peaked at 1.5 ng/mL on day 1 (mean 0.8 ng/mL across three days), while C7-PQS, C9-PQS, HHQ and HQNO ranged 0.5-1.5 ng/mL. Although the cohort is too small for biomarker validation, the study provides first proof-of-concept that *A. baumannii*-associated AHLs are quantifiable in human plasma during sepsis. Earlier tandem-MS detection of QS activity in clinical isolates provided the technical basis for sample preparation [Chan_2014]. As a biomarker proposition, QS molecules could inform anti-virulence-dosing decisions, but concentration thresholds and severity-score correlations are not yet established.

### 3.6.2 Genomic epidemiology of QS gene variation in CRAB

Clinical-isolate sequencing studies are abundant. Lebreton sequenced 167 *A. baumannii* and 93 *P. aeruginosa* isolates from Ukrainian patients, identifying related XDR clones (ST-2/blaOXA-23, ST-78/blaOXA-72, ST-400/blaGES-11); *P. aeruginosa* ST-773/ST-1047 carried LasR loss-of-function mutations, whereas the *A. baumannii* AbaI/AbaR locus did not, consistent with its conserved hospital-reservoir role [Lebreton_2025]. Nghiem found ST2 (39.3%) and ST571 (21.4%) as dominant Vietnamese lineages, with *abaI* and *abaR* frequently co-localised with blaOXA-23 on AbaR4b islands — evidence for joint dissemination of resistance and QS determinants on mobile elements [Nghiem_2025]. Zhang confirmed a near-uniform ST2/blaOXA-23 background and conserved biofilm-formation genes across 92 Chinese CRAB isolates [Zhang_2025]. Further contributions document AbaR4 islands in companion-animal isolates [Chanchaithong_2025], CRAB global clone 2 geographic spread [Kim_2013], susceptible-isolate biofilm paradoxes [Wiradiputra_2025], and Iranian ICU genotyping [Azimi_2025]. Colonisers showed stronger biofilm and desiccation tolerance than pathogenic isolates [Sharma_2025]. *abaI*- and *csuE*-positive isolates had higher MDR rates across 14 antibiotic classes [Liu_2016_2; Tang_2020]. Fernandez-Garcia demonstrated significantly lower *aidA* expression (P < 0.001) in isolates causing bacteraemic versus non-bacteraemic pneumonia — the first direct linkage between QS regulatory-gene expression and clinical syndrome [FernandezGarcia_2018]. Whole-blood ex vivo data showed up-regulation of iron-acquisition, *abaI* and *csu* operons during the bloodstream phase [Ghavanloughajar_2025]. AbaI/AbaR and *aidA* are largely conserved across globally important CRAB lineages; expression rather than presence is the most likely modulator of clinical phenotype.

### 3.6.3 Compassionate use and clinical trials

Direct human therapeutic experience is restricted to phage compassionate use. The Schooley case reactivated phage compassionate-use protocols in the West. No QSI for *A. baumannii* identified here has progressed beyond Phase I evaluation. The Beasley QSI-antibiotic enhancement preprint highlights repurposed drugs as candidate combinations but explicitly states that further preclinical investigation is required [Beasley_2025]. No registered ClinicalTrials.gov record for an *A. baumannii*-targeted QSI was identified within this corpus.

### 3.6.4 The clinical translation gap

Phage cocktails are furthest advanced, supported by compassionate-use precedent, preclinical pneumonia and burn-wound studies [Wienhold_2021; deVilliersdelaNoue_2025], ex vivo human-lung confirmation and colistin synergy; no licensed phage product yet exists for *A. baumannii*. Small-molecule QSIs are less advanced, evaluated only in single-laboratory in vivo studies without PK characterisation [Cui_2025; Khafagy_2025; Nosair_2025]. QQ enzymes remain at the in vitro to early in vivo stage [Vogel_2022; Mayer_2020]. The dominance of conserved ST2/ST571 lineages with intact *abaI/abaR* machinery [Nghiem_2025; Zhang_2025; Lebreton_2025] suggests broad strain coverage is in principle achievable, while the clinical-syndrome-to-gene-expression linkage [FernandezGarcia_2018] indicates biomarker stratification using the Carpenito analytical platform [Carpenito_2025] will likely be required.
