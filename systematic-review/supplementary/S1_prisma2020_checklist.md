## SWiM (Synthesis Without Meta-analysis) Reporting Compliance

The narrative synthesis (Sections 3.3, 3.4, 3.7, 3.8) follows the SWiM guideline (Campbell M, et al. *BMJ* 2020;368:l6890):

- Item 1 (Grouping studies): by intervention class and study design (§3.4)
- Item 2 (Standardised metric): effect sizes harmonised to % change or fold-change where possible (§3.8)
- Item 3 (Synthesis method): tabulation + narrative with effect-size summaries
- Item 4 (Heterogeneity exploration): §3.9.3
- Item 5 (Certainty of evidence): narrative assessment in §4 (heterogeneity precluded formal GRADE)
- Item 6 (Data presentation): structured tables (Tables 1–3), text-based figures (Figures 1–2)
- Item 7 (Limitations): §3.9, §4.6
- Item 8 (Authors' description of synthesis approach): §2.8, §3.8
- Item 9 (Reporting of the synthesis): Sections 3.3–3.8

*This checklist accompanies the integrated PubMed + OpenAlex + Crossref synthesis described in §2.3. Heterogeneity in outcome metrics, sub-MIC dosing and assay formats precluded a formal pooled meta-analysis and GRADE certainty grading; the implications of this scope are addressed in §4.6 (Limitations) and motivate the minimum reporting dataset proposed in §3.9.5 (Box 1).*
